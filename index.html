  4. overview of Sydney living areas

    1. North shore of Sydney (North Sydney to Hornsby) is generally the most expensive and quite safe and decent.
    2. North west of Sydney (Pennant Hills to Castle Hills) is considered the next best if you have high school going children
    3. West (Parramatta to Mt Druitt, includes Blacktown) is convenient for new migrants with young families and has Affordable housing. This is where most Indian temples and Indians are.
    4. South of Parramatta (Granville Merrylands to Campsie) is full of migrants from Arab and Africa. Decent place to live if mosques are a priority.
    5. Southern suburbs (Airport to Hellensburg) is again decent and affordable.
    6. Newtown and around is popular with alernative life styles e.g. Greenies, Gay.

  10. withdraw your EPF in five simple steps

    Step1: First login to the UAN portal with your details.

    Step 2: Check the status of your KYC details.

    Step 3: Select the withdrawal form which applies to your situation; PF full withdrawal (in case you are unemployed for more than two months), EPS (pension) withdrawal benefits or EPF advance (partial withdrawals for education, marriage expense, house purchase etc)

    Step 4: To submit the form you will need to feed in the authentication OTP, which is sent on your mobile number. This mobile should be the only which is linked to both your UAN as well as Aadhaar.

    Step 5: The authorities will get your e-KYC (Aadhaar) credentials from UIDAI and processes your online PF claim.

    Keep in mind that there are certain conditions which you need to fulfill you apply for the EPFO withdrawals online. You should have activated your UAN. Your mobile number should be linked to it. Your aaadhaar and bank details as well as PAN should be seeded on your EPFO.

  2. Driving License

    If you hold Indian permanent driving license, it is valid for only 3 months from your date of arrival.(every state has a different rules so check before you fly. Victoria needs RTO letter, so make sure you get one before coming here)

    After that, you need to have Australian state license. Generally there are 4 stages in driving license here, learner license, provisional 1, provisional 2 and then unrestricted permanent license.

    But if Indian license is more than 3 years old, you can skip provisional 1 and 2. This means only learner (for minimum 1 month) and then directly jump on unrestricted permanent license.

    For learner license, you need to book appointment online and there is an online test at their centres. There will be 45 questions asked in 1 hour (objective questions).

    You need to have learner license for minimum 1 month before you apply for permanent license.

    For permanent license, again you need to book an appointment and there is practical driving test for 45 minutes in city traffic. The instructor will be in your car and he will assess your driving skill on different points. No other person is allowed in the car during test.

    More details

    Guide to driving licence - NSW

  5. Health Insurance
    Don't register for Medicare if you aren't returning for permanent settlement within a year

    Lifetime Health Cover is a Government initiative that started on 1 July 2000. It was designed to encourage people to take out hospital insurance earlier in life, and to maintain their cover.
    In Australia, private health insurance is not ‘risk-rated’ like most forms of insurance. Private health insurers cannot refuse to insure any person, and must charge everyone the same premium for the same level of cover, despite their risk profile and likelihood of using health services.

    LHC is a financial loading that can be payable in addition to the base rate premium for your private health insurance hospital cover. If you purchase hospital cover earlier in life and keep it, you will pay lower premiums compared to someone who joins when they are older.

    LHC loadings apply only to hospital cover. They do not apply to private health insurance general treatment cover (also known as ancillary or extras cover).

    To avoid paying a LHC loading, you need to purchase hospital cover by 1 July following your 31st birthday. If you purchase hospital cover after this date you may be required to pay a LHC loading – 2% for each year you are over 30. For instance, if you wait until you are 40, you could be paying an extra 20% on the cost of your hospital cover. If you wait until you are 50, you could pay 40% more. And so on, up to a maximum of 70% more.

    If you are a new migrant to Australia and are aged 31 or over, you will not have to pay a LHC loading if you purchase hospital cover within 12 months of being registered for full Medicare benefits.

    *If you delay purchasing hospital cover for more than 12 months after your registration, you will have to pay 2% more for each year you are aged over 30 when you purchase your hospital cover*.
    Any LHC loading that you pay will be removed from your hospital cover premium after you have held hospital cover for 10 continuous years. However, if you cancel your hospital cover after the LHC loading has been removed, you may become liable to pay a LHC loading again in the future.

    If you were born on or before 1 July 1934, you are exempt from the LHC loading. You can purchase hospital cover at any time and pay the base rate premium.

    In some cases, calculating your LHC loading can be fairly complicated. The Private Health Insurance Ombudsman (PHIO) has developed a Lifetime Health Cover Calculator, which will help you to work out your LHC loading based on your answers to a series of questions.

  6. Rebate

    To be eligible for the rebate, permanent residency is must.

    After that you register yourself or your partner with the Centrelink body (under dept of human services) to claim child care expenses. Also register child.

    Depending on the facility, their do a direct debit of 50% of the fees from your account and get paid in the background the remaining 50%.

    Child care benefit which is additional to rebate is income tested and has a threshold. That changes from FY to FY. When you register for Centrelink benefits you will be asked to submit an income forecast for the year (combined) and accordingly advised if or not you are eligible of the benefit.

  1. Child care Rebate

    Centrelink is the govt. Agency which gives social benefits. Get yourself and your kid registered there.
    Centrelink gives 2 things - Child Care Rebate and Child Care Benefit. CCR is 50% of per day daycare fees upto a max of $7500 per yeae.
    CCB is income tested ane accordingly given to you.
    There is another thing too - called Family Tax Part B which is for families with kids. This one is income tested too.

  2. Childcare Rebate (called CCR) and Childcare Benefit (CCB)

    Now, nationally all parents (to my knowledge) are eligible for 2 things - Childcare Rebate (called CCR) and Childcare Benefit (CCB) these two are aids for all parents. Only those who are below a certain combined income can claim both. Most are eligible for at least 1 of these. The ccr provides 50% of childcare expense up to $7000 per year back and the ccb provides a reduced child care fee. - so this is the money aspect of it. All this info ia available on the Centrelink website which is easily accessible too and has a calculator for eligibility etc

    In terms of the rest- depending on where you live or which childcare you choose the availability and prices vary... all childcare (99.5%) charge a daily fee I. E. 1 fixed amount for the full day... now a full day for childcare is 6am to 6pm but it's up to the parents when they want to pick up the child... earlier than 12 hrs.

    Another option for child care is Family Day Care, which i found very unprofessional and is basically a day care run at home by a certified educator who has approval from council. I didn't find fdc helpful or likeable for my daughter so it's not something I recommend.

    Selection Criteria for when selecting the centre:
    1. Timing - the childcare drop n pick up timing was suitable to me n hubby as we both work.
    2. Staff - were the staff friendly or seemed grumpy... did other kids recognise them? How did they interact with kids during the tour?
    3. Cleanliness- basic hygiene and cleanliness that you can see.
    4. Flexibility - for food habits etc ...
    5. Protocol - in case of injury or medicine required.

    School Year is from JAN to DEC and is divided into 4 terms. Typically each term is of 10 or 11 weeks, followed by school holidays for 2 weeks, then again next Term and so on
    Primary School is from Kindergarden to Year 6, High School is from Year 7 to Year 12
    Year 4 - there is an Exam called OC - Opportunity Classes
    Year 6 - There is an important exam called Selective. This is an entrance exams for admission to Selective High Schools
